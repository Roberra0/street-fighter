**Basic Rules and Knowledge #1**

you can win a round in two ways.

1. Defeat the opponent within the time limit 2. Have more health than your opponent when the time limit is up.

When the round begins, both fighters start with maximum health, and any health lost in the previous round is regained for the new round. While the length of the health bar is the same for all characters, each character has a set amount of health.

A perfect occurs when someone wins a round without losing any health at all. there should be a recognitiion in this case.



**The Basics of Universal Movement**
Dashes
Make two inputs directly forwards or backwards and the character will perform a quick movement. Forward is forward dashing, while backwards is back dashing. In

-- Walking backwards is usually slower than walking forwards.

There are three movement states - standing (walking), crouching, and jumping.

**Categories of States**
 we can divide the states into 4 major categories.

Attacking
Regular: Standing, crouching, jumping. Does not include attacking.
Blocking: Upon blocking an attack.
Getting Hit: Upon getting hit with an attack.

In any Standing or crouching state, If you get hit, your character will be knocked backwards while keeping their feet in place. This is called hit stun. There is a different hit stun for standing and crouching. If you can hit someone who is in a hit stun with another attack during this period, they will not be able to block it. Successive attacks linked together in this way are called "combos".


In mid jump, If you get hit while in mid-air, your character will lay horizontally mid air and hit the floor with a double thud then get up.



**Hour 4: Basic Rules and Knowledge: Offense**
There are Normal, unique, and special moves, and throws.

Normal Moves
- heavy punch (q key)
- mid punch (a key)
- heavy kick (w key)
- Mid kick (s key)
- Each of the movement states gets their own normal attack variation (attacks performed while walking are the same as standing). For example standing mid punch, courching mid punch, jumping mid punch.   

  
Unique moves require commands, but are limited to one stick direction and one button. For example, forward + heavy punch. Unique moves exist between normal moves and special moves. Unlike normal moves they can move your character while attacking, making for a very refined move.

Heavy attacks deal more damage, but as they are bigger movements, they will leave you more vulnerable if you miss.


Throws are a special close-range attack where you grab your opponent and inflict damage on them. Like normal moves, this is an attack that all characters have by default, and as we'll talk about in the defense lecture coming up, this is an attack that can defeat your opponent's guard.


**Basic Rules and Knowledge: Defense**
Input the joystick in the direction away from your opponent in order to block.
Input the joystick in the direction opposite of your opponent, and when you are attacked you will block automatically."


That "automatically" is very important - without it, if you constantly input back on the joystick, then your character would just continually block.

Some attacks that can't be blocked:
Normal/Unique Moves: no damage upon blocking
Special Moves: 50%  damage upon blocking
Throws: cannot be blocked.

Your character can be in three basic states - standing, crouching, or jumping. you can only block while standing or crouching. You cannot block in mid-air.

While blocking, throws do not work against you.

Blocking while in the crouching state is a crouch block. Input the joystick in the down-back position to perform a crouch block.

States Where you cannot Block
In addition to jumping, there are other states where you won't be able to perform a block.

You will not be able to block until the move has finished.
This is what makes missing heavy attacks so dangerous.
Getting hit with an attack will cause your character to stagger or be knocked down before returning to original standing state. You'll be unable to move while in that staggering or knocked down state and are vulnerable to consequent attacks.

Collision Boxes
This is a special type of box, whose function is to keep two characters from overlapping each other. If your opponent is right next to you and they start walking, they'll push you backwards.


**Attack Frame Mechanics**
There are 3 parts to an attack.

1 Startup
2 Attack Active Frames
3 Recovery Period
It's possible for 1 attack to have multiple attack active frames, but the general flow is basically the same. We'll get into more details about that later.

Startup
The time period from when the attack starts, up until the active frames begin, is called "startup." The fewer startup frames there are, the faster the attack will come out. Attacks with fast startup usually cannot be beaten, so it's okay to think in terms of fast = strong.


Attack Active Frames
The time during which the attack motion has a hitbox is called the "attack active frames." You can also refer to this period as just "active frames" or "active." In this column I'd like to use the notation "active frames." your chracter is immune to attacks while in attack active frame


Recovery
"Recovery period" refers to the period after the active frames have ended, until your character can move again. Simply put, it's the time when you're open to attack. This can also be referred to as "recovery time" or just "recovery."
